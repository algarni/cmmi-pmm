Main Description

General Overview

The objective of this policy is to communicate a path of actions that constitute an PM initiative based on the experiences the TETCO  Service management Quality Assurance (QA) has gained while working with the TETCO  respective Service management related departments and organization units.

 

This policy describes the Service management Process Improvement model, PM, which can be used to guide development of a long-range, integrated plan for initiating and managing an PM program. The purpose of this policy is to provide PMO managers comprehensive rules and procedures representation of the TETCO  Service management Process Improvement initiation, planning and implementation.

 

 

Lifecycle Model Overview

The model shown in the below figure depicts the major lifecycle phases of an Service management Process Improvement (PM) initiative, which provide a continuous loop through the steps necessary for the PM.

 

It is important to note that the length of time it takes to complete a cycle through the PM model will vary from project to project and from organization unit to organization unit at TETCO .

 

 

Organization unit will find, depending on the resources they are able to commit to the PM, many activities that can be pursued in a parallel fashion. There will also be instances of some parts of the organization pursuing activities in one phase of the model while others are pursuing activities in a different phase. In practice the boundaries between the phases of the PM are not as clearly defined as shown in the model.

 

It is also important to note that the infrastructure set up to accomplish PM will play a significant role in the success or failure of the PM initiative. The value that the infrastructure brings to the PM initiative, its understanding of its roles and responsibilities, cannot be underestimated.

PM Activities

The purpose of the PM process model is to guide TETCO  organizations with planning and implementing an effective Service management process improvement projects through performing the following activities:

 

Describing TETCO  Service management Current Business

The purpose of this activity is not to describe the entire TETCO  applications organization in detail-only enough of it to allow the team to prioritize which parts of the organization to focus on for the Service management development organization. The reasons for doing business modeling should guide the breadth and depth of the effort. Based on the business-modeling objectives, the processes are prioritized, and the Process View of the Business Architecture Document is updated with the architecturally significant processes. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Reviewing and Analyzing Historical Data

- Outlining the High-Level Target Organization

- Describing Forces and Objectives Affecting the Business Architecture

- Preparing the Business Architecture Document

- Obtaining Approval to the Business Architecture Document

 

 

Alignment of Organizational Objectives to Measures

 


1. Identification of Organizational Objectives or KPIs.
2. Relative Ranking of the KPIs, based on Senior Management Inputs.
3. Identification of related Quality and Process Performance Objectives, which are directly aligned to the Organizational Objectives.
4. Key Measures and Metrics, which are outputs for measuring Performance and Quality Objectives in projects are identified from the list of available measures in the organization. New Measures, if needed, to indicate performance have also been introduced.
5. Related Weightages have been set for each output measure to the QPPO, which is in turn linked to the Organizational Objectives.
6. The List of all parameters which affect the Output Measures / Metric have been identified for the applicable lifecycles.



 

 Tracking of Organizational Objectives :

The alignment of the Organizational Objectives to the measures, is defined . The performance of the Organizational Objectives shall be done once in 6 Months. This shall be derived from the actual performance of projects and functions, rolling up to the Organizational objectives.
This would cover the current performance against the Organizations Objectives. The risks if any, for meeting the Organization's objectives would be highlighted here. Actions for performance improvement would be documented for tracking.

 

Assessing TETCO  Service management Development Processes & Analyzing Process Improvement Opportunities

Process assessments (appraisals) are performed for the following reasons:

- To identify processes that should be improved

- To confirm progress and make the benefits of process improvement visible.

- To satisfy the needs of a TETCO -customer relationship.

- To motivate and facilitate buy-in.

- To understand the driving forces.

- To identify and document the objectives of Gap Analysis.

- To define the scope of Gap Analysis (i.e. each process and improvement scope).

 

The buy-in gained during a process appraisal can be eroded significantly if it is not followed by an appraisal-based action plan. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Obtaining Sponsorship of the Process Appraisal

- Defining the Scope of the Process Appraisal

- Determining the Method and Criteria for Process Appraisal

- Planning, Scheduling, and Preparing for the Process Appraisal

- Conducting the Process Appraisal

- Documenting and Delivering the Appraisal’s Activities and Findings

 

Establishing Process Improvement Action Plan

The purpose of this activity is to determine and prioritize the candidate process improvements as well as the process improvements that will be implemented. This activity places by establishing and maintaining process improvement action plan which is targeting specific improvements that have been defined to address weaknesses uncovered by appraisals. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Identify Process Improvement Objectives

- Identify Process Areas to be improved

- Identify Involved Organization Units

- Identify Resources for the Required Roles and Responsibilities

- Identify Potential Risks

- Prepare the Process Improvement Action Plan

- Review and Update the Lessons Learned

- Obtain Commitment to the Process Improvement Action Plan (Presentation, Meeting or Mail)

 

Maintaining Process Definition

The purpose of this activity is to provide guidance and support for the members of the TETCO  improvement initiatives. "Information at your fingertips" is a metaphor that aligns well with the purpose of this work product. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Update Process Asset Library

- Share the Process Asset Library to Obtain Commitment

 

Obtaining Commitment on Process Definition

The purpose of this activity is to formally verify that the results of the process definition tasks conform to the TETCO  requirements as well as the reference model standards. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Schedule a Workshop for Process Definition (If Needed)

- Conduct the Process Workshop (If Needed)

- Obtain Approval and Prepare Process Definition Compliance Document

 

Establishing Process Pilot Implementation (If Needed)

Pilot projects are used to test out the solutions in both the process-centered and problem-centered approaches. The solutions will require some tailoring and refinement to fit them into projects across the development organization units, and the pilots will help determine the tailoring needs and guidelines for the rest of the organization. During pilots execution the process group held periodical meetings for problems shooting and monitor progress against pilot project schedules. Also issues addressed during pilot-run were reported to the process group’s schedule for reviewing each pilot project was specified. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Prepare and Assign Process Improvement Pilot Project Team

- Train Pilot Project Team

- Install the Approved Solution in the Pilot Project

- Execute and Monitor the Pilot Project

- Capture Lessons Learned from the Piloting

Rolling-Out Process Definitions

This activity is designed to officially generalize the new process across the Service management development departments used by the TETCO _PMO. This activity should include at least the following procedures:

- Review the Pilot Implementation Results

- Inform the Service management Development Related Departments of the New Process

- Publish the New Process Asset Library

Configuration Identification

 

The work products and deliverables of the project that shall be brought under configuration management depend on the type and size of project. The Configuration Controller needs to identify the CI’s. There are two groups of CI’s based on control levels:

 

A. Controlled Group

 

To control configuration item (CI’s), each must be uniquely identified. This requires the following:

- A naming convention by which a CI can be uniquely identified

- A description which defines the characteristics of the CI

- The point at which the baseline of a CI is established

- The location where the CI is to be stored

 

Include the following:

1. Configuration Management Plan

2. Process Asset library

3. Business Architecture Document

4. Gap Analysis Finding Briefing

5. Process Improvement Action Plan

6. Process Definition Compliance

Approval on all Controlled CIs taken by mail from PMO ( If no feedback within 2 dyes this will be approval ) .

 

B. Managed

 

To manage configuration item (CI’s), each must be uniquely identified. This requires the following:

- A naming convention by which a CI can be uniquely identified

- The location where the CI is to be stored

Including the following:

1. Tasks

2. Issues

3. Lessons Learned

4. Workspaces

5. Project Schedule

6. Reviews

7. Risk Lists

8. Project Status Reports

9. Configuration Audit Reports

10. Process Audit Reports

11. Source Control

12. Process Improvement Proposals (PIPs)

Organizational Measures


 

1.      Resource Productivity Rate:


 

This measure shows the average hours of effort per function point. This represents the effort needed by TETCO team to deliver one function point. This measure is the basis for planning  (estimating) the effort needed for any new typical project. The measure is calculated as average of the productivity rate of all TETCO typical projects completed so far.


 

Productivity Rate (Organization) = AVERAGE [ Productivity Rate (Projects) ]


 

Where


 

Productivity Rate (Projects) = Project Effort (In Man Hours) / Project Size (In Function Points)


 

Where Project Effort is collected from the Actual Work in the project Schedule of all project tasks at the end of the project, and Project Size is collected from the last baseline version of the project Business Case.


 

2.      Phase Effort Percentage



 

This measure provides PMs with the percentage of effort of each project phase (refer to the definition of the project lifecycle) compared to the total project effort. These percentages are used for estimating effort of new projects in the Business Case.


 

Phase Effort Percentage = Phase Effort / Project Effort
