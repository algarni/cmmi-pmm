    Main Description

    he person playing the Configuration Controller role should understand configuration management principles, and preferably have experience or-at a minimum training-in the use of Configuration Management tools.

     

    The responsibilities of the Configuration Controller can be detailed as follows:

    1.             Overall responsibility for the configuration management process

    2.             Managing new versions of processes, release packaging, project builds and publish them after getting the required approvals.

    3.             Keeping   configuration management systems up to date.

    4.             Communicating efficiently with software quality manager, process engineers and EPG team.

    5.             Planning, monitoring, executing and reporting of entire CM plan.

    6.             Ensure the goals of the   quality improvement projects are met through  _PM policies and process.

    7.             Ensuring that configurations contain all their intended parts and are sound with respect to their specifying documents, work items including requirements and architectural specifications.

    8.             Providing accurate status and current configuration data to process engineers and project stakeholders.

    9.             Identifying and managing configuration items, components, and related work products to be placed under configuration management.

    10.      Establishing and maintaining the   configuration management and change management systems  for controlling work products.

    11.      Creating, releasing and managing baselines for internal use and for delivery to the stakeholders.

    12.      Tracking and controlling change requests for configuration items.

    Perform configuration audits to maintain the integrity of configuration baselines

     
    Staffing
    Skills

    The person playing the Configuration Controller role should understand configuration management principles, and preferably have experience or-at a minimum training-in the use of the Configuration Management tools. 

     

    The Configuration Controller role is played best when the practitioner pays attention to detail. They should be assertive, in order to ensure that developers do not bypass configuration management policies and procedures.
